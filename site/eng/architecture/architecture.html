Architecture

Harmony between nature and architecture

Kyoto's traditional buildings, such as Buddhist temples and Shinto shrines, are designed to blend harmoniously into their natural surroundings. The use of local materials (wood, bamboo, stone) and the layout of the gardens reflect this philosophy.

A refined wabi-sabi aesthetic

The architecture reflects the principles of wabi-sabi (the beauty of imperfection and simplicity) and great attention to detail. The refined interiors, the play of natural light, the sliding doors (shōji) and the tatami mats illustrate this sober refinement.

Preserving traditional skills

Kyoto is a model for the preservation of historic buildings, thanks to the rigorous transmission of artisanal techniques (carpentry, kawara tile roofing, lacquerware, etc.). This cultural continuity ensures the durability and authenticity of its architectural heritage.
